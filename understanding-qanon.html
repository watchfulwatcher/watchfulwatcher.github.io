Understanding the QAnon Movement

by Watchful Watcher - 2/18/2020

As the QAnon movement continues to grow into a worldwide phenomenon, many still don't understand what exactly the movement is, and how it's changing the world.


To understand QAnon, we must investigate all aspects of the movement. What is QAnon, who is Q, what is Q's motivation, what is Q posting, and how is the Q movement changing the world? Like Q says, "Think for yourself, Sheep no more".




What is QAnon?

QAnon stands for Q Anonymous, a movement centered around an anonymous 8chan user named Q, and the community that follows Q posts, commonly referred to as "drops". QAnons use social media to share research, while encouraging others to investigate and think for themselves.



Who is Q?

Q began posting on the controversial imageboard website 4chan in October 2017 but can now be found on 8kun, also known as Inifintychan, a free speech version of 4chan.

Q claims to have a Q clearance or Q access authorization, which is the Department of Energy (DOE) security clearance required to access Top Secret Restricted Data, Formerly Restricted Data, and National Security Information, as well as Secret Restricted Data.

Q hasn't revealed his identity to the public (yet), but there are clues to help QAnons speculate on who Q is. Q often drops pictures of various locations and items, such as an airport or a watch. Based on these pictures, some QAnons believe Q may be in Trump's Administration. Other QAnons think Q might be General Michael Flynn, because Flynn frequently follows QAnons on Twitter.

Q Drops

Q drops update QAnons on, "The Greatest Military Intelligence Operation of Our Time". According to Q, the US Army, under the direction of Trump's administration, is taking down a global satanic cabal that secretly controls America. Q drops usually give coded updates on this military operation, while encourage QAnons to find the truth through research. Sometimes, Q drops feature a QAnon tweet, which encourages QAnons to interact and share research.




The Satanic Cabal Conspiracy

The Satanic Cabal Conspiracy was first popularized in the 80s by FBI agent Ted Gunderson, who uncovered The Finders, a large and well organized satanic cult that Mr. Gunderson claims is a covert CIA front involved in the international trafficking of children. Recently declassified FBI documents reveal that the State Department, in conjunction with the FBI's Counterintelligence Section, conspired to, and successfully covered up The Finders investigation.




Ted Gunderson was an American Federal Bureau of Investigation Special Agent In Charge and head of the Los Angeles FBI. After retiring from the FBI in 1979, he was asked to investigate the Jeffrey R. MacDonald case as a private investigator. Doctor MacDonald was a Green Beret who had been convicted of the 1970 murders of his pregnant wife and two daughters.


"I've established beyond any question of a doubt that this man is absolutely innocent, and on October 25 1980, obtained a signed confession from Helena Stoeckley" - Ted Gunderson


During Gunderson's investigation he obtained a signed confession from Helena Stoeckley. In Stoeckley's confession, she admits that the murders were carried out by her satanic cult group, and that it was her initiation into the cult that night. She had detailed information about the inside of the house, including a jewelry box in the master bedroom, and a rocking horse that had a broken spring in the child's bedroom.


In March of 1981 Ted Gunderson submitted a 1100+ page report to the FBI and the DOJ detailing his findings. Much to his surprise, nothing was done, in fact, the FBI buried the case. Later Gunderson would state, "I Noticed evidence was lost, destroyed, stolen, there were strong indications of corruption". This cover-up pushed Gunderson down the rabbit hole, leading him to eventually uncover The Finders, and document the CIA's involvement.





Mainstream Media's QAnon Narrative

The mainstream media claim QAnon is a baseless conspiracy theory that is influencing the 2020 Presidential Election, and may lead to violent extremists. Their collective narrative suggests Q is an anonymous Live Action Role-Play troll. On May 1st 2019 Trump retweeted 20 QAnons in one day, resulting in the mainstream media criticizing Trump for facilitating dangerous conspiracy theories.




FBI Memo on QAnon

In reaction to the QAnon phenomenon, the FBI released a memo that labeled the QAnon movement a fringe political conspiracy theory that is very likely to motivate some domestic extremists to commit criminal, sometimes violent behavior.




Former CIA Counter Terrorism, Counterintelligence and Staff Investigator Kevin Shipp made a reaction video to the FBI memo. In it, he stating that there really is a Cabal that controls America, and they attempted a well documented soft coup against President Trump. Mr. Shipp goes on to say that the FBI memo unfairly covers millions of Americans, many of which, are peaceful people.

The QAnon movement is encouraging millions of people around the world to do their own research and question everything. After decades of mainstream media outlets spewing fake news to controlling the destiny of mankind, regular people are finally starting to wake up and search for the truth. Uniting under the idea that we, the people, are the now news.

The truth is out there, but it's up to each person to do their own research, and draw their own conclusions. Together, we can debate, share ideas, and discover the truth.
